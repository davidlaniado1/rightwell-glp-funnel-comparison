RightWell Intake Quiz — Build Spec (Short-Form)

v2.0 · 2026-06-12 · 12 screens (11 for men) · ~2 minutes Owner: Growth. Audience: design, engineering, compliance, care-ops.

Guided health check, not paperwork. All mandatory clinical fields collected. No "approved/qualified/guaranteed" language anywhere. No fake urgency. Provider decides — always.

Global rules: persistent header = logo + progress bar + trust strip ("Reviewed by licensed providers · Private & secure"). One screen = one moment (may hold 2–3 related quick fields). Single-selects auto-advance. Sticky Continue elsewhere. 🔒 "Your answers are private and protected" footer on data screens.

1. THE 12 SCREENS

1 · Start

  • Headline: A clearer path to medical weight management.
  • Sub: A few questions help a licensed provider review whether the RightWell program may be appropriate for you.
  • Trust line: Provider-reviewed · Private & secure · About 2 minutes
  • CTA: Start my health check →
  • Compliance: No qualify/approved/guarantee language. ✔
  • API: create intake_session_id.

2 · Your goal (3 quick taps, one screen)

  • Headline: Let's start with your goal.
  • Why we ask: Your goals help the care team understand what you're working toward.
  • Fields:
  • Main goal: Weight loss · Gain muscle · Maintain my current body → primary_goal
  • Why now: Live longer · Look and feel better · Reduce current health issues · All of these → primary_reason
  • Tried a weight management program before? Yes · No → prior_weight_loss_program
  • Validation: all 3 required. Error: "Please answer each one to continue."
  • Design: three compact pill-rows, Continue activates when all answered.

3 · About your body

  • Headline: A few basics about you.
  • Why we ask: Height, weight, and sex assigned at birth help a provider review your information accurately.
  • Fields: Height (ft 3–7 / in 0–11 dropdowns) → height_feet,height_inches · Weight in lbs (number, 50–700) → weight_lbs · Sex assigned at birth: Male / Female → gender
  • Reassurance: Estimates are fine. No judgment — this is just a starting point.
  • Errors: "Please enter your height." / "Please enter your weight in pounds." / "Please select an option."
  • Logic: gender gates Screen 5. BMI computed server-side; never show eligibility verdicts.

4 · Vitals

  • Headline: Two quick health numbers.
  • Why we ask: Blood pressure and heart rate are simple safety checks for your provider. Best estimates are fine.
  • Fields:
  • Blood pressure: <120/80 (normal) · 120–129/<80 (elevated) · 130–139/80–89 (high st.1) · ≥140/90 (high st.2) · I don't know → blood_pressure_range
  • Resting heart rate: <60 (slow) · 60–100 (normal) · 101–110 (slightly fast) · >110 (fast) · I don't know → resting_heart_rate_range
  • Validation: both required ("I don't know" allowed — flags provider follow-up; confirm with clinical partner). Error: "Please pick the closest option — 'I don't know' is okay."

5 · Safety check (women only)

  • Headline: Safety first — do any of these apply?
  • Why we ask: These protect your safety during provider review.
  • Multi-select, "None" exclusive: Currently or possibly pregnant · Breastfeeding or bottle-feeding with breastmilk · Gave birth within the last 6 months · None of the above → pregnancy_status[]
  • Logic: Male → skip, auto-set not_applicable. Error: "Select all that apply, or 'None of the above.'"

6 · Health conditions — serious

  • Transition banner (top of screen, 1 line): Next: health questions so a provider can review your information safely. No right or wrong answers — just accurate ones.
  • Headline: Do any of these apply to you?
  • Multi-select, "None" exclusive (verbatim list): End-stage kidney disease (on or about to be on dialysis) · End-stage liver disease / cirrhosis · Current suicidal thoughts or prior suicidal attempt · Active cancer — diagnosis, treatment, or remission within last 5 years (excl. non-melanoma skin cancer) · Organ transplant on anti-rejection medication · Severe GI conditions (gastroparesis, blockage, inflammatory bowel disease) · Current treatment for alcohol, opioid, or substance use disorder · None of the above → severe_conditions[]
  • Design: if suicidality selected, show quiet 988 support line (copy via legal/clinical). Error: "Select any that apply, or 'None of the above.'"

7 · Health conditions — history

  • Headline: Any current or past conditions from this list?
  • Helper: This helps providers understand your health history. Most people select at least one.
  • Multi-select, "None" exclusive (verbatim list): Active gallbladder disease · Hypertension · Sleep apnea · Type 2 diabetes (not on insulin) · Type 2 diabetes (on insulin) · Type 1 diabetes · Diabetic retinopathy · Blood thinner use (warfarin/Coumadin/Jantoven) · Pancreatitis (history or current) · Personal/family history of MTC · High cholesterol or triglycerides · Severe depression · Liver disease incl. fatty liver · Congestive heart failure · Urinary stress incontinence · PCOS · Clinically proven low testosterone · Osteoarthritis · None of the above → other_conditions[]
  • Design: visually group (heart/metabolic · digestive · hormonal · other); sticky Continue; never paginate.

8 · Medications (both questions, one screen)

  • Headline: Quick medication check.
  • Why we ask: Providers check this to avoid interactions and plan a safe starting point.
  • Fields:
  • Currently taking any medications (incl. weight loss meds or supplements)? Yes/No → current_medications; if Yes → textarea "List name, dose if known, reason if known" (placeholder: Lisinopril 10mg — blood pressure) → current_medications_detail
  • Taken any weight loss medication in the past month? Yes/No → recent_wl_medication; if Yes → textarea "Name, dose if known, when you last took it" (placeholder: semaglutide 0.5mg — 2 weeks ago) → recent_wl_medication_detail
  • Validation: both y/n required; textareas required-if-yes (≥2 chars). Error: "Please list at least the medication name — your provider needs this for a safe review."

9 · Optional context (one screen, all skippable)

  • Headline: Optional — anything else that would help?
  • Fields:
  • What matters most when reviewing options? (max 2): Affordability · Potency · Side effect management → preferences[]
  • How are you feeling about your goals? I'm ready · I'm feeling hopeful · I'm cautious → motivation_level
  • Anything else for the provider? textarea ≤1,000 chars (sleep, symptoms, concerns, goals) → provider_notes
  • CTA: Continue → · Skip link: Skip this step
  • Note: standalone sleep + energy/clarity screens cut; sleep is mentioned in the textarea helper.

10 · Review + consent (value bridge built in)

  • Headline: Quick review before you submit.
  • Bridge line (top): RightWell organizes your answers for licensed provider review — your answers help the care team understand what options may be appropriate.
  • Summary: collapsed sections (Goals · Body basics · Health · Medications) with Edit links that return here.
  • Consent checkbox (verbatim, required): "I confirm my answers are accurate to the best of my knowledge. I consent to the collection and secure transmission of my health information for review by a licensed provider. I understand that submitting does not guarantee eligibility, approval, prescription, or treatment."
  • Disclaimer (displayed, verbatim): "This information is for educational purposes only and is not medical advice. Always consult a licensed healthcare provider."
  • CTA: Submit for provider review → (disabled until server confirms all mandatory fields + consent)
  • API: consent_given + timestamp + consent_v; single POST of full payload on submit.

11 · Thank you / next steps

  • Headline: You're done — here's what happens next.
  • Body: 1) We organize your information for provider review. 2) A licensed provider or care team reviews it and may reach out for more information. 3) You'll hear from us with your next step.
  • Expectation line (verbatim): Submitting does not guarantee eligibility, a prescription, or treatment. Treatment decisions are made by licensed providers where applicable. No medication ships before review is complete.
  • Support: Questions? Our care team: [support email/phone].
  • CTA (if offer follows): See how the RightWell program works →

12 · Offer bridge (only if payment follows)

Here's how the RightWell program works. • A licensed provider reviews your information; if treatment is appropriate, they decide the plan. • Membership covers: provider review and visits, ongoing care-team support, dosage guidance, shipping coordination. (State only what's actually included.) • Not guaranteed: eligibility, a prescription, or specific results. If a provider determines treatment isn't appropriate, [state true refund/no-charge policy]. • Cancel per the plan terms shown before payment. CTA: See plans → - Real promos with real end dates only. No counters, no expiring "approvals," no "you are approved" before provider review.

Off-ramp template (only if clinical partner supplies hard-stop rules — never improvise): "Thanks for being upfront — that keeps you safe. Based on your answers, this program may not be appropriate right now. That's a safety decision, not a judgment. We recommend speaking with your healthcare provider." No selling on this screen.

2. FIELDS, VALIDATION & LOGIC

3 height_feet, height_inches / weight_lbs / gender int / int / enum Y 3–7, 0–11 / 50–700 / m,f
4 blood_pressure_range / resting_heart_rate_range enum Y 5 options incl. unknown
5 pregnancy_status[] enum[] Y (F only) ≥1, "none" exclusive; M → not_applicable

Conditional logic: gender=M skips Screen 5 · Yes reveals med textareas · "None of the above" exclusive both directions · suicidality select shows 988 line (non-blocking) · partner hard-stops route to off-ramp · submit enabled only after server-side completeness check.

3. ERROR LIBRARY

Empty select: "Please choose an option to continue." · Empty multi: "Select any that apply, or 'None of the above.'" · Height/weight: see Screen 3 · Med detail: "Please list at least the medication name — your provider needs this for a safe review." · Consent: "Please review and confirm the consent statement to submit." · Submit fail: "We couldn't submit just now — your answers are saved. Please try again." (answers persisted locally, retry w/ backoff, ops alert after N failures; never show raw API errors).

4. API & DATA NOTES

Single POST at Screen 10 only — no partial health-data submission. Sandbox + production keys via config; keys only in the secrets manager (never Drive/sheets/screenshots/design files/docs/repo). Payload = snake_case fields above + session id, consent version/timestamp, non-health UTM/click IDs. Verify 2xx + record ID; show confirmation ID on Screen 11. Abandonment analytics = screen numbers + timestamps only, zero health answers; nothing health-related to ad platforms without legal approval; follow-up contact only with proper consent.

5. MOBILE UX

390px-first · option buttons ≥56px full-width · auto-advance singles · sticky Continue above keyboard · numeric keypad for weight · long checklists grouped, never split across screens · progress bar never visually resets on skips · trust strip on every screen.

6. COMPLIANCE CANON

  • Consent + disclaimer copy: Screen 10 verbatim blocks (also disclaimer on 11).
  • Expectation line: "Submission does not guarantee eligibility, treatment, or prescription. A licensed provider makes treatment decisions where applicable." (Screens 10, 11, 12.)
  • Banned words in any headline/CTA: approved, qualified, guaranteed, prescribed (QA greps copy).
  • No BMI verdicts, no medication-included implication unless true, no weight-loss numbers, no fake urgency/scarcity.
  • Posture supports HIPAA-style safeguards + LegitScript review.

8. RISKS & WINS

Friction risks: back-to-back checklists (6–7) — mitigated by grouping + reassurance; weight-entry shame — "no judgment" line, no BMI verdicts; free-text meds — placeholders show minimum needed. Compliance risks: "approved" leakage (grep gate) · implying meds included (offer bridge states true list) · health data in pixels (engineering gate + audit) · improvised off-ramp copy (template + legal sign-off only). Conversion wins vs current intake: 18 → 12 screens · trust strip everywhere · why-we-ask on every clinical screen · review+consent step (cleaner data, less anxiety) · value bridge folded into review (no extra screen).
